Projects

I've worked on some neat things.
Intern storage and tracking for Mitzvah Circle Foundation

For my part 1 capstone course, we work in groups of 4 and we work directly with a client. The client being the Mitzvah Circle Foundation, they are a nonprofit who helps families in need. They hire many interns to help with the process, but did not have a great way of storing and managing them. All their data was manually entered into an excel spreadsheet. My group and I are now designing a system to better store and track interns within their system. We are currently in the design process and will move on to implementation in the next semester.

He is currently creating a software for any contractors to use, plumbers, HVAC, landscaping, etc. It was a lot of work so he was looking for people to bring in so I helped and worked on the project. I was exposed to using Angular, Typescript, and plenty of useful modern day design principles for both code and UI/UX.

Mechanical Hand Sign Language

Thinking about it now, this was way out of our league. In highschool we did a class project for my AP computer science class of 8 people. we decided to do a mechanical hand that worked through motors to sign several different letters, and eventually you would be able to speak a word to it and it would sign the word. I was on the team to work with the motors and get them to work. It was a fun process to work with C and get a raspberry PI to communicate and turn rotors. Other teams worked on models of a hand, speed and turning of motors needed to make hand gestures, using google APIs for speech to text. We were not able to complete the project, but we had a hand hooked up to motors and strings that could sign the letter A.
